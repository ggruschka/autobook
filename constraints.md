# Book Constraints

Everything in this file is a hard constraint. The agent must satisfy all of them in every iteration. Anything NOT specified here is up to the agent's creative interpretation.

## Required

- **Title**: *El Bosque de las Tres Puertas*
- **Genre**: Fantasy — fairy tale (cuento de hadas)
- **Target Audience**: Children aged 5, read aloud by a parent. No dumbing down — children are capable of understanding real stories with real stakes.
- **Approximate Length**: ~3,000–5,000 words (short story / fairy tale length, comparable to an original Grimm tale)

## Optional

- **Writing Style**: Authentic Brothers Grimm style — formal fairy tale prose, rhythmic, with the cadence of oral storytelling. Not saccharine, not condescending. The narrative voice should trust the child's intelligence. Dark for today's standards but timeless.
- **Point of View**: Third person omniscient — the classic fairy tale narrator who sees all and occasionally addresses the reader.
- **Setting**: Classic European — medieval forests, villages, perhaps a castle or a cottage. The world of Grimm: deep woods, stone paths, candlelight.
- **Themes**: Consequences — actions have real, lasting consequences, as in original fairy tales. Subtle traditional Catholic references woven naturally into the fabric of the story (not preachy, not didactic — the way faith permeates medieval European life).
- **Tone**: Gently dark — real danger, real stakes, a touch of menace. Beauty and terror coexist, as in the best fairy tales. Not sanitized, but not gratuitous.
- **Structure**: Classic three-act (setup, confrontation, resolution).
- **Characters**:
  - **Guille**: A boy. Short curly brown hair, brown eyes, skinny. The protagonist or co-protagonist.
  - **Isa**: Guille's sister. Golden blonde hair, green eyes, pale white skin. Co-protagonist.
  - Additional characters at the agent's discretion — antagonists, helpers, magical creatures — as the story requires.
- **Moral/Ethical Guidelines**: The story should embody both traditional and classical virtues: prudence, justice, fortitude, temperance, obedience, humility, honesty, and reverence. Virtue should be rewarded and vice should have consequences — but not in a heavy-handed way. The moral should emerge from the story, not be stapled onto it.
- **Content Warnings**: Keep it Grimm-authentic. Real stakes including death are permitted. No sanitizing, no Disney-fying. Trust the tradition.
- **Reference Works**: Brothers Grimm originals (*Kinder- und Hausmärchen*, the unedited versions), Hans Christian Andersen, Charles Perrault.
- **Language**: Spanish
