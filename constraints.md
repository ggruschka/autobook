# Book Constraints

Everything in this file is a hard constraint. The agent must satisfy all of them in every iteration. Anything NOT specified here is up to the agent's creative interpretation.

## Required

- **Title**: La vida de Padre Pio
- **Genre**: Historical fiction
- **Target Audience**: Boys, ages 5–15. Assumes no prior knowledge of Padre Pio or Catholic theology. Stories should captivate young readers with adventure, wonder, and moral clarity.
- **Approximate Length**: ~30 pages (~5,000–7,000 words, ~8–12 chapters)

## Optional

- **Writing Style**: Brothers Grimm style — fairy-tale narration with vivid imagery, moral weight, and a timeless storytelling voice. Direct, evocative, never condescending.
- **Point of View**: Omniscient narrator
- **Setting**: Italy, late 19th and 20th century — Pietrelcina (childhood), San Giovanni Rotondo (adult life). Blend historical detail with a storybook atmosphere.
- **Themes**: Faith, sacrifice, obedience, spiritual warfare, miracles, suffering as redemption, humility before God.
- **Tone**: Reverent but engaging — wonder and gravity, not dryness. Moments of warmth, moments of awe, moments of genuine danger (spiritual and physical).
- **Structure**: Linear narrative following Padre Pio's life chronologically, from birth to death, with key episodes as standalone chapters that also build a larger arc.
- **Characters**: Francesco Forgione (Padre Pio), his family (especially his devout mother), fellow friars, the faithful who sought him, and spiritual adversaries (the devil, appearing in vivid but age-appropriate ways).
- **Moral/Ethical Guidelines**: Reinforce traditional Catholic values — prayer, obedience, humility, charity, the reality of good and evil, the power of the sacraments (especially Confession and the Eucharist). Present suffering as meaningful, not gratuitous. The devil is real and fearsome but always defeated by faith. No moral relativism.
- **Content Warnings**: Spiritual combat scenes should be vivid but not traumatic for younger readers. The stigmata should be presented with reverence, not gore.
- **Reference Works**: Brothers Grimm fairy tales (narrative voice), *The Little Flowers of St. Francis* (tone and episodic structure), children's hagiographies.
- **Language**: Spanish
