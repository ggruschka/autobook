# Book Constraints

Everything in this file is a hard constraint. The agent must satisfy all of them in every iteration. Anything NOT specified here is up to the agent's creative interpretation.

## Required

- **Title**: Padre Pío
- **Series**: Mi primer Santoral (a collection of saints' lives for children)
- **Genre**: Historical fiction / hagiography
- **Target Audience**: Children ages 8-12. Catholic families. Readers who are curious about the lives of saints.
- **Approximate Length**: ~15,000 words, ~5-7 chapters

## Optional

- **Writing Style**: Direct and vivid, with sensory richness. Grimm brothers-level maturity — never condescending, never dumbed down. Trust the reader's intelligence.
- **Point of View**: Third person omniscient
- **Setting**: Italy, from the late 19th century through the mid-20th century. Pietrelcina (childhood), San Giovanni Rotondo (adult life). Includes World War I and World War II periods.
- **Themes**: Faith and doubt, obedience and humility, the redemptive meaning of suffering, prayer as communion with God, charity toward the poor and sick, the reality of spiritual warfare.
- **Tone**: Reverent but honest. Wonder without sentimentality. The miraculous presented matter-of-factly, as saints' lives have always been told.
- **Structure**: Linear narrative following Padre Pío's life chronologically.
- **Characters**: Padre Pío (Francesco Forgione, 1887-1968) as the central historical figure. Supporting cast drawn from real people in his life: his parents (Grazio and Maria Giuseppa), fellow friars, spiritual children, and the doctors and Church officials who investigated him.
- **Moral/Ethical Guidelines**: Traditional Catholic values. Faith, prayer, obedience, humility, charity. Suffering as redemptive and meaningful, not pointless. The sacraments — especially confession and the Eucharist — presented with reverence. Obedience to the Church even when the Church is unjust.
- **Content Warnings**: The stigmata, physical suffering, spiritual attacks, and wartime violence are presented honestly and age-appropriately. Do not sanitize or euphemize — children can handle hard truths told well. Do not dwell gratuitously on gore.
- **Reference Works**: Old-school hagiographic tradition (Butler's Lives of the Saints, Golden Legend) adapted for children. Grimm brothers-level willingness to include darkness and real stakes.
- **Language**: Spanish
